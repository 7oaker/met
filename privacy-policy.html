2. Scope and Purpose of Data Processing

Met is a personal relationship management app ("CRM") designed for offline, on-device use. Its purpose is to help you remember people you meet — at events, conferences, or in everyday life — by storing their contact details, voice notes, photos, and interaction history on your device.

Data about third parties (people you add to Met)

When you use Met, you store personal data about other people (contacts). Under GDPR Art. 2(2)(c) and Recital 18, the regulation does not apply to processing of personal data carried out by a natural person in the course of a purely personal or household activity. If you use Met solely for personal networking (not in a professional capacity on behalf of an organisation), you act as the data controller for your contacts' data, and the GDPR household exemption applies.

If you use Met in a professional context (e.g., as a sole trader, founder, or sales professional), you are the data controller for the contact data and are responsible for ensuring a lawful basis for storing that data. Met's architecture — fully offline, no shared database, no cloud sync — is designed to minimise risk for all users.

4. How Data Is Collected

4.1 Manual Entry

You type contact information directly into the app. No data is sent anywhere during this process.

4.2 Business Card Scan

Met can photograph a physical business card and extract text from it. This uses Google ML Kit Text Recognition, which runs entirely on your device — the image is never uploaded to any server. After extraction, the text is parsed locally to populate contact fields. The card photo is stored on your device if you choose to keep it.

4.3 LinkedIn QR Code Scan

Met can scan a LinkedIn QR code to extract a LinkedIn profile URL. The camera captures the QR code locally; the URL is decoded on-device. No data is sent to LinkedIn or any other service as part of this scan.

4.4 Phone Contacts Import

With your explicit permission, Met can read contacts from your device's address book (name, phone numbers, email addresses, company, job title). Imported contacts are stored in Met's local database on your device. No contacts data is uploaded to our servers. You can undo an import from Settings → Remove last import.

4.5 Voice Recording

Met can record a voice note about a person or an interaction. The audio is saved locally in WAV format inside the app's private storage directory. The microphone is only active while you are actively recording; Met does not record in the background.

4.6 On-Device Transcription (Whisper AI)

If you enable voice note transcription in Settings, Met downloads an open-source speech recognition model (OpenAI Whisper, via the ggerganov/whisper.cpp repository on Hugging Face) onto your device. Once downloaded, transcription is performed entirely on-device — your voice recordings are never sent to any server. Three quality tiers are available (Tiny ~75 MB, Base ~150 MB, Small ~490 MB), stored in the app's private file storage. During the model download, only the model file itself is transferred; no identifying information is sent to Hugging Face.

4.7 Face Photo / Photo Library

You may attach a face photo to a contact by taking a new photo with the camera or selecting one from your photo library. This requires camera or photo library permission. Photos are stored in the app's private storage on your device and are not uploaded anywhere.

6. Optional Webhook Integration

Webhook is disabled by default. It must be explicitly enabled and configured by you in Settings → Webhook.

Met offers an optional webhook feature that sends contact data to an HTTPS URL of your choice (e.g., a Zapier zap, Make scenario, or your own server) when you add or edit a contact.

When enabled, the following data may be transmitted to the URL you configure:

  • Name, job title, company
  • Email address, phone number, LinkedIn URL, website, address
  • Category tags
  • Associated event name
  • Text notes and voice note transcript
  • Follow-up flag and date met
  • An internal contact ID and timestamp

You are solely responsible for the security and privacy compliance of the endpoint URL you configure. Voice audio files are never transmitted by the webhook — only the text transcript (if transcription is enabled) is included. You can disable or remove the webhook at any time in Settings.

If you send contact data to a third-party service (e.g., Zapier, Make, Notion), that service's own privacy policy applies to data once received.

8. Data Storage and Security

All data is stored on your device using the following mechanisms:

  • App database: Contact records, events, interactions, and settings are stored using AsyncStorage, serialised as JSON in the app's sandboxed private directory.
  • Audio files: Voice note WAV files are stored in <documentDirectory>/voice-notes/ inside the app sandbox.
  • Whisper model files: AI model binary files are stored in <documentDirectory>/whisper/.
  • Photos: Face and business card photos are stored in the app's private storage directory.

The app sandbox on iOS and Android provides hardware-level encryption (iOS uses the Secure Enclave; Android uses file-based encryption on supported devices). Met does not implement additional application-level encryption on top of OS-level protections, which are enabled by default on all modern iOS and Android devices.

Met does not use iCloud backup, Google Drive backup, or any other cloud sync. If you back up your device to iCloud or Google, the app data may be included in that backup — this is governed by Apple's or Google's privacy policy, not ours.

9. Data Retention

Data is retained indefinitely until you delete it. You are in full control:

  • Delete a contact: Swipe to delete or use the delete button on the contact profile. This removes the contact record and its associated voice note audio file.
  • Delete an interaction: Remove individual interaction logs from a contact's profile.
  • Remove a voice note: Tap the trash icon in the voice recorder to delete the audio file.
  • Undo contact import: Settings → Remove last import deletes all contacts imported in your most recent import.
  • Delete all data: Uninstalling the app removes all stored data from your device (including voice note files and model files), subject to your OS backup settings.

10. Data Export and Portability

You can export all your contacts as a CSV file via Settings → Export all data (DSGVO Art. 20 – Right to data portability). The export includes all structured contact fields and is generated entirely on your device. You choose where to save or send the file using your device's share sheet. We do not receive a copy of the export.

12. Your Rights Under DSGVO

Under the DSGVO / GDPR you have the following rights regarding any personal data about you that we process as data controller:

  • Art. 15 – Right of access: You may request confirmation of whether we process personal data about you and, if so, a copy of that data.
  • Art. 16 – Right to rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate personal data we hold about you.
  • Art. 17 – Right to erasure ("Right to be forgotten"): You may request deletion of personal data we hold about you where there is no legitimate ground for continued processing.
  • Art. 18 – Right to restriction of processing: You may request that we restrict processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Art. 20 – Right to data portability: You may request your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Art. 21 – Right to object: You may object to processing based on legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)).
  • Art. 7(3) – Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

Because Met stores all data locally on your device and we have no access to it, the most effective way to exercise rights over contact data is directly within the app (editing, deleting). For any data that we might hold about you as the app user (for example, if you contact us by email), please reach out to ks.siebeneicher@gmail.com.
